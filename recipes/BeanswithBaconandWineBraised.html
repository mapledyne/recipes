Beans with Bacon and Wine, Braised

Category : Beans, Side Dish

Description:


Prep Time: 15 Min
Cook time: 1 H 30 Min
Servings:

Ingredients:
1/2 pound bacon Nueske's brand. Cherrywood smoked, green package (exact), diced
1 onion, large, peeled and diced
2 celery stalks, diced
2 carrots, medium, peeled and diced
4 garlic cloves, chopped
2 large sprigs rosemary
1 pound beans, dried (pinto or white beans)
2 teaspoons salt, coarse, more to taste
2 cups red wine, dry
olive oil, extra-virgin, for serving

Instructions:
1. In the bottom of a large enameled cast-iron pot, over medium-high heat, brown bacon until golden, about 5 minutes. Stir in onion, celery, carrots, garlic and rosemary. Cook, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are tender, 5 to 7 minutes.
2. If you pre-soaked the beans, drain them and add to pot along with the salt. Pour in enough water to just cover the beans (about 7 to 8 cups). Bring liquid to a boil; reduce heat and simmer gently until beans are just tender, 45 minutes to 1 hour for soaked beans, 1 1/2 to 2 hours for unsoaked beans.
3. Meanwhile, in a small pot over medium heat, simmer wine until it is reduced to 2/3 cup, 20 to 30 minutes.
4. Remove rosemary branches from bean pot and discard them. Pour wine into beans and cook for 10-15 minutes longer to meld flavors and thicken broth to taste. Drizzle with olive oil and serve, preferably with some crusty bread.
