Chicken Salad, with Almonds and Peppadews

Category : Christine Friendly, Fancy, Lunch, Meat

Description:
A few years ago, Toby Cecchini published a tuna salad recipe in the New York Times that was unlike any I'd seen before. Divorced of mayonnaise, the tuna was left in large slivers, dressed with lemon and oil, and mixed with pepperoncini, dill, parsley and smoked almonds. There wasn't a scrap of celery in sight. The tuna held its own between lashings of pepper and flourishes of dill, and seemed like so much more than just tuna salad. I wondered: why not apply the same method to chicken? I like a good mayonnaise-based chicken salad, but one with more candid flavors would be a great recipe to have around for when chicken salad and a glass of white wine feels like the perfect weekend lunch.

Prep Time: 20 Min
Cook time:
Servings: 4

Ingredients:
1/4 cup onion, red, thinly sliced
salt
4 cups chicken, cubed roasted
3 tablespoons peppers, peppadew, slivered
1 cup artichoke hearts, marinated
1/4 cup almonds, smoked, roughly chopped
2 teaspoons thyme, chopped
1 tablespoon mustard, whole-grain, plus more to taste
1 tablespoon vinegar, sherry
Lemon juice, to taste
1/4 cup olive oil, extra virgin
black pepper, freshly ground

Instructions:
1. Sprinkle the onion with salt and toss to coat. Let sit for 15 minutes. Squeeze the onions to drain any juices.
2. In a bowl, toss together the onion, chicken, peppadews, artichoke hearts and almonds. In a small bowl, whisk together the thyme, mustard and vinegar. Gradually whisk in the oil. Season with salt and pepper. Pour the dressing over the chicken and fold together. Squeeze in lemon juice to taste -- it will take a fair amount, probably 1/2 to 1 lemon. Let sit for 15 minutes. Taste and adjust the seasonings. Serve.
