Carmelized Nectarines with Honeyed Ricotta and Quinoa Crunch

Category : Breakfast

Description:


Prep Time:
Cook time:
Servings: 4

Ingredients:
1 cup quinoa, rinsed and drained well
1/2 cup almonds, sliced, raw
3 tablespoons sesame seeds, raw
1 tablespoon maple syrup, pure
1 tablespoon safflower or canola oil
15 ounces ricotta, part-skim
2 tablespoons honey, liquid
1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract [#8144]
1/4 teaspoon lemon zest, grated
2 tablespoons olive oil, extra virgin
2 tablespoons honey, liquid
4 (about 1 1 /4 pounds) nectarines, ripe, halved and pitted
1 tablespoon orange juice, fresh

Instructions:
To make quinoa crunch: Preheat the oven to 375 degrees. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone mat.
Mix the quinoa, almons, sesame seeds, maple syrup and oil and spread on the prepared baking sheet. Bake until toasty and fragrant, 10 to 12 minutes.
Transfer to a bowl and cool completely. Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 month.
To make the honeyed ricotta: In a small bowl, use a whisk to whip the ricotta, honey, vanilla and lemon zest together until light and creamy, 1 to 2 minutes. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
To make the nectarines: Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. When the oil is shimmering hot, add the honey and stir to combine. Put the nectarines, cut-side down, in the pan. Decrease the heat to medium and cook until the bottoms begin to brown and the flesh softens, 8 to 10 minutes, scotting the fruit halves around in the pan periodicallye to ensure they're not sticking. During this time, the honey will bubble up and darken. Decrease the heat to low and add the orange jucie. Swirl the pan to distribute the juices evenly. Gently flip the nectarines. Continues cooking for 5 minutes, then remove from heat.
To serve, place one nectarine half on each plate, spoon a large dollop of honeyed ricotta on each, and generously sprinkle wiht quinoa crunch. Serve immediately.
