Butter Tarts, Gooey

Category : Dessert

Description:
A Canadian favourite

Prep Time:
Cook time:
Servings: 12

Ingredients:
1 1/2 cups flour, all-purpose
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/4 cup butter, cold, cubed
1/4 cup shortening, cubed
1 egg yolk
1 teaspoon vinegar
Filling:
1/2 cup sugar, brown, packed
1/2 cup corn syrup
1 egg
2 tablespoons butter, softened
1 teaspoons vanilla
1 teaspoons vinegar
pinch of salt
1/4 cup raisins, currants, pecans or shredded coconut

Instructions:
1. In large bowl, combine flour with salt. With pastry blender or 2 knives, cut in butter and shortening until in fine crumbs. In 1-cup liquid measure, whisk egg yolk with vinegar; add enough ice water to make 1/3 cup. Gradually sprinkle over flour mixture, stirring briskly with fork until pastry holds together. Gather dough; press into disc. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or until chilled.
2. Filling: In bowl, vigorously whisk brown sugar, corn syrup, egg, butter, vanilla, vinegar and salt. Set aside.
3. On lightly floured surface, roll out pastry to 1/8-inch thickness. Using 4-inch round cookie cutter, cut out 12 circles, rerolling scraps if necessary. Fit into 2 3/4 x 1 1/4 inch muffin cups. Divide filling solid of choice (currant or raisin or pecans or shredded coconut) among pastry shells. Spoon in filling until three quarters full.
4. Bake in bottom third of 450 degree oven for about 12 minutes or until filling is puffed and bubble and pastry is golden. Let stand on rack for 1 minute. To prevent sticking, immediately run metal spatula around tarts to loosen; carefully slide spatula under tarts and transfer to rack and let cool.
Variations:
Drizzled - Drizzle cooled tarts with semi-sweet or white chocolate, melted.
Not-so-gooey - Increase brown sugar to 3/4 cups; decrease corn syrup to 1/4 cup.
Chocolate butter tarts - Reduce all-purpose flour in pastry to 1 1/4 cups and sift into bowl along with 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder. Continue with recipe. To filling, add 1/4 cup chopped bittersweet chocolate instead of currants, raisins, pecans or coconut.
