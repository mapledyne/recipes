Nanaimo Bars

Category : Dessert

Description:
A Canadian favourite. Very sweet, so cut into truffle-sized squares.

Prep Time:
Cook time:
Servings:

Ingredients:
1 cup butter, melted
2/3 cup cocoa powder
1/2 cup sugar, granulated
2 eggs, lightly beaten
3 cups graham cracker crumbs
2 cups coconut, shredded
1 cup walnuts, finely chopped
Filling:
1/3 cup butter, melted
1/4 cup milk
2 teaspoons vanilla
4 cups sugar, powdered
Topping:
8 ounces chocolate, semi-sweet
2 tablespoons butter

Instructions:
1. Grease 13 x 9 inch metal cake pan and line with parchment paper, leaving 1 inch extending over long edges for handles. Set aside.
2. In large bowl, whisk together butter, cocoa powder, sugar and eggs; stir in cracker crumbs, coconut and walnuts. Press evenly into prepared pan. Bake in the center of a 350 degree oven for 10 minutes. Let cool in pan on rack.
3. Filling: In large bowl, stir together butter, milk and vanilla; beat in sugar until thickened and smooth. Spread evenly over cooled base. Refrigerate for about 45 minutes or until firm.
4. Topping: Meanwhile, chop chocolate; in heatproof bowl over saucepan of hot (not boiling) water, melt with butter. Spread evenly over filling. Refrigerate until set. Using parchment paper handles, lift out of pan. Peel off paper. Cut into bars. (Make-ahead: Cover and store at room temperature for up to 2 days or overwrap with heavy-duty foil and freeze for up to 2 weeks).
Variations:
Topsy-Turvy - For filling, decrease milk to 2 tablespoons and icing sugar to 1 1/3 cups; add 2/3 cup cocoa powder. For topping, substitute 10 ounces white chocolate for semi-sweet chocolate.
Peanut Butter - For filling, increase milk to 1/3 cup and decrease icing sugar to 1 1/2 cups; add 1 cup smooth peanut butter.
Orange - For filling, substitute 1/4 cup orange liqueur or orange juice concentrate for milk and add 1 tablespoon of grated orange rind.
