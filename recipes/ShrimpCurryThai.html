Shrimp Curry, Thai

Category : Grain or Rice Dishes, Kid Friendly, Meat, Weeknight

Description:


Prep Time: 5 Min
Cook time: 20 Min
Servings: 4

Ingredients:
14 oz can coconut milk, full-fat (Mae Ploy brand preferred)
2 tablespoons curry paste, green (Mae Ploy brand preferred)
1 tablespoon sugar, palm (or brown)
1 tablespoon fish sauce
1 pound shrimp, large, peeled
to taste basil leaves, fresh
1 tablespoon lime juice, fresh
1 1/2 cups rice, basmati brown, to serve
vegetables, frozen, (optional)

Instructions:
1. If you think of it, refrigerate the can of coconut milk overnight. This helps it separate into thin and thick milk. Open the can and pour off the watery, thin coconut milk into a bowl. Add the thick, chunky coconut milk to the saucepan and bring to a boil over medium-high heat. (If your coconut milk doesn't want to separate, don't worry about it; just pour half in the saucepan and reserve half).
2. Add the curry paste and stir to dissolve. Cook until the coconut milk is thick and fragrant and hisses when you stir, about 3 minutes. (The coconut milk may begin to separate into oil and solids; that is not a problem). Add the vegetables and heat through if using. Add the palm sugar, fish sauce and reserved thin coconut milk and bring to a boil, stirring frequently, until the shrimp is just opaque throughout, 1 1/2 to 3 minutes (Test by removing a shrimp and cutting it in half).
3. Off the heat, stir in the basil leaves and lime juice and serve with rice.
