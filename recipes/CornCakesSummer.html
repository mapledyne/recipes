Corn Cakes, Summer

Category : Appetizer, Kid Friendly, Veggie Side, Vegetarian Main Dish, Weeknight

Description:
I first tasted this corn cake at Kerry Diamond's quaintly Southern restaurant Seersucker, where it was strewn with a tangle of raw corn kernels and heirloom tomatoes -- and that's what you should do to it too. (Two seasonal birds, one stone.) The thing lasted a mere 3 minutes on the table, and around minute 1, it occurred to me that this was the only responsible way to navigate corn fatigue: let the sweet, starchy kernels stage a little healthy competition with basil and red onion, and then mellow it all out in a hot, buttered skillet. If you're wise, you'll top it with a poached egg, like Seersucker does. A fried one works just as well, so long as there's a runny yolk to mediate between flavors, to make them place even nicer than they're told they should. Recipe adapted from Seersucker.

Prep Time: 22 Min
Cook time: 8 Min
Servings: 4

Ingredients:
2 cups (3 ears) corn kernels, divided in half
2 tablespoons butter, plus more for cooking
1/2 cup buttermilk
1/2 cup onion, red, finely diced
1/2 cup basil, chiffonade plus more for garnish
3/4 cup flour, all-purpose
1/2 cup cornmeal
1 teaspoon baking powder
1/2 teaspoon baking soda
Pinch of salt and pepper
2 tomatoes, heirloom, diced
1/2 cup corn kernels
2 tablespoons olive oil
Salt and pepper
eggs, (optional) poached or fried or left off if you're lazy

Instructions:
1. In a blender, pur√©e half the corn kernels, butter, and buttermilk until mostly smooth. Transfer to a bowl. Mix in remaining corn kernels, onion, and basil.
2. In a separate bowl, thoroughly combine all of the dry ingredients.
3. Add wet ingredients to the dry, and mix until just combined.
4. In a skillet, melt some butter over medium heat and about add 2 heaping tablespoons of batter for each pancake. (You can customize this based on how big you'd like your cakes to be, of course.) Cook about 2 minutes per side or until cooked through. Season with salt and pepper and set aside.
5. For the topping, combine tomato and corn with olive oil and a pinch of salt and pepper. Spoon mixture over the corn cakes.
6. Top each cake with an egg, salt, and pepper, and garnish with basil.
