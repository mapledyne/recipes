Tagliatelle with Summer Squash Ragu

Category : Pasta, Vegetarian Main Dish, Weeknight

Description:


Prep Time:
Cook time:
Servings:

Ingredients:
3 cloves fresh garlic
1/4 cup olive oil
2 tablespoons fresh marjoram, , picked from stem not chopped
2 tablespoons thyme, fresh, , picked from stem not chopped
1 small pinch red chili flakes (optional)
2 pounds zucchini, coarsely grated
2 teaspoons kosher salt
4 ounces water
3 tablespoons white balsamic vinegar
1 pound fresh or dried tagliatelle
3 tablespoons butter, salted
1/4 cup parmesan, grated

Instructions:
Smash the garlic cloves completely with the side of a knife and add to a cold saucepan, along with the olive oil. Place the pan over a medium-low flame. Do not brown the garlic. Once the garlic has begun to sizzle, add the fresh herbs and the chili flakes and continue to gently simmer for one minute.
Add the grated zucchini, salt and water, and increase the flame to medium-high. Let the zucchini simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally until the squash have begun to break down. Meanwhile, put a large pot of well-salted water on to boil, for the pasta.
Add the vinegar to the zucchini, stir, and taste for seasoning, adding more salt if desired.
Cook pasta until it still has a little crunch to it; youâ€™ll finish cooking it to al dente in the zucchini sauce. When you drain the pasta, reserve a tablespoon of the water it was cooked in.
Cut the butter up into small pats, and toss into the sauce, along with the pasta and the reserved pasta cooking water. Stir over low heat for about 30 seconds, until the pasta finishes cooking and the sauce begins to stick to the noodles.
Top with grated parmesan and serve.
