Fontina, Baked

Category : Appetizer

Description:
My Baked Fontina recipe has become a Super Bowl tradition. There's something about a big skillet of gooey, melted cheese - with plenty of crusty bread for dipping- that gets everyone on the same team. And the best part is, it takes less than 10 minutes to make, so you don't have to stand in the kitchen cooking while everyone is watching the game!

Prep Time: 5 Min
Cook time: 10 Min
Servings: 5

Ingredients:
1 1/2 pounds cheese, Italian Fontina Val d’Aosta cheese, rind removed and 1-inch-diced
1/4 cup olive oil, extra-virgin
6 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
1 tablespoon thyme leaves, fresh, minced
1 teaspoon rosemary leaves, fresh, minced
1 teaspoon salt, kosher
1 teaspoon black pepper, freshly ground
1 bread, crusty French baguette

Instructions:
Preheat the broiler and position the oven rack 5 inches from the heat.
Distribute the cubes of Fontina evenly in a 12-inch cast-iron pan. Drizzle on the olive oil. Combine the garlic, thyme, and rosemary and sprinkle it over the cheese and olive oil. Sprinkle with the salt and pepper and place the pan under the broiler for 6 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbling and starts to brown.
Serve the baked Fontina family-style—right out of the oven in the cast-iron pan with crusty chunks of bread for everyone to dip.
