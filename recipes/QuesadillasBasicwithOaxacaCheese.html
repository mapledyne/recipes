Quesadillas, Basic with Oaxaca Cheese

Category : Kid Friendly, Vegetarian Main Dish, Weeknight

Description:


Prep Time:
Cook time:
Servings:

Ingredients:
tortillas, flour
cheese, Oaxaca, grated
olive oil, extra virgin
filling (mushrooms, green onions, black olives, diced tomatoes, cooked chicken, avocado)

Instructions:
Heat a large cast iron frying pan to medium-high heat. Add a small amount of oil (or butter) and spread around the bottom of the pan. Take one large flour tortilla and place it on the pan. Flip the tortilla over a few times, 10 seconds between each flip. Air pockets should begin to form within the tortilla.
When pockets of air begin to form, take a handful of grated cheese, sprinkle over the top of the tortilla, making sure that the cheese does not land on the pan itself. Add whatever additional ingredients you choose, but take care not to layer the ingredients too thickly.
Reduce the heat to low and cover the pan. The cast iron pan shock be hot enough by now to have plenty of residual heat to melt the cheese and brow the tortilla. After a minute, check to see if the cheese is melted. If not, return the cover and keep checking until the cheese is melted.
When the cheese is sufficiently melted, use a spatula to lift up one site of the quesadilla and flip over the other side, as if you were making an omelet. The tortilla should be browned slightly. If it is not browned, turn the heat up to high and flip the quesadilla over every 10 seconds or so until it is.
Remove from pan and cut into wedges. Serve with lettuce, salsa, sour cream, and guacamole.
