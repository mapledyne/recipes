Pancakes, Whole Grain Buttermilk

Category : Breakfast

Description:


Prep Time:
Cook time:
Servings: 6

Ingredients:
1 cup flour, all-purpose
1/3 cup flour, buckwheat
1/4 cup spelt flour
1/3 cup oats, rolled
4 1/2 teaspoons sugar
1 tablespoon baking powder
1 teaspoon baking soda
salt, kosher, Diamond Crystal brand (exact)
2 eggs, large
1 cup milk
1 cup buttermilk
2 tablespoons butter, unsalted, melted and cooled
for serving maple syrup, pure

Instructions:
In a large bowl, stir together all the dry ingredients.
In a small bowl, whisk together the egg, milk, buttermilk, and butter. Whisk the dry ingredients into the milk mixture until smooth. Let the batter rest for 10 minutes to allow the dry ingredients to soak up some of the liquid. If the batter feels too thick (difficult to easily whisk or stir), and 1 tablespoon more milk or water to loosen it up.
Melt a nub of butter in a large skillet over medium heat (you want the melted butter to completely coat the pan). Scoop 1/4 cup of batter into the pan. Repeat, depending on the size of your pan. Cook each pancake until the bottom turns golden brown and the top begins to bubble, 2 to 3 minutes. Flip and cook the other side an additional 1 to 2 minutes. You'll likely need to add more butter to the pan in between batches.
Serve warm, topped with butter, honey or maple syrup.
Batter can be made ahead for up to one day. Add milk or water to loosen it after it sits overnight.
