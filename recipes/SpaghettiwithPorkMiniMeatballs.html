Spaghetti, with Pork Mini-Meatballs

Category : Kid Friendly, Pasta

Description:


Prep Time: 10 Min
Cook time: 30 Min
Servings: 4

Ingredients:
For the meatballs:
2 slices bread, white sandwich, crusts removed, torn into pieces
1/2 cup milk
1 egg, large, beaten
2 ounces cheese, Parmagiano Reggiano, grated
2 tablespoons oregano, fresh, minced
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 teaspoons salt, kosher, Diamond Crystal brand (exact)
to taste pepper
1 lb pork, ground
For the sauce:
2 tablespoons olive oil, extra-virgin
2 cloves garlic, minced
28 oz can tomatoes with juice
1 tablespoon tomato paste
1 teaspoons sugar
to taste salt and black pepper
1 lb spaghetti pasta, whole wheat

Instructions:
1. In a bowl, combine the bread and milk and mash with a fork until it forms a paste. Stir in the egg, cheese, oregano, garlic, salt and pepper. Add the pork and mix (hands work well) until well combined.
2. Heat the olive oil in a large nonstick skillet or cast iron pot over medium-high. Drop 1 tablespoon dollops of meat mixture into the skillet (the meat mixture will be too soft to form meatballs ahead of time, and they'll come out a bit like thick sausage patties. Yes, they're supposed to be like that). Working in two batches, brown the meatballs on two sides, about 2 minutes per side, and transfer to a plate.
3. Add the remaining garlic and cook until fragrant, about 15 seconds. Add the tomatoes, tomato paste and sugar and bring to a boil. Cover over medium-high 10 minutes, until thickened, reducing heat if the sauce spatters too much. Return the meatballs to the pan, reduce heat to medium-low, and cover. Cook for 10 minutes or until the meatballs are cooked through, stirring occasionally. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
4. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente. Combine the pasta, sauce and meatballs and serve.
