Toast, Thin Crisp

Category : Appetizer, Kid Friendly, Snacks

Description:


Prep Time: 3 Min
Cook time: 7 Min
Servings: 3

Ingredients:
bread, stale, sliced very thin
olive oil, extra-virgin, sprinkled on pan
salt
toppings (see notes)

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Slice the bread very thin (to make sure it gets crunchy, but is tender and non mouth-injuring). Easiest to slice thin if the loaf is stale.
2. Sprinkle olive oil onto a baking sheet, then lay out the thinly sliced bread. Sprinkle or brush the slices with more oil and a little salt and shuffle, rubbing them together and soaking up whatever oil is in the pan.
3. Rearrange the slices so they're all in a single layer again and toast for 7 minutes. Turn the pan front to back and toast for a few more minutes, until the toasts are golden brown all over. They will probably not all be done at the same time, so remove the ones that are first.
4. Break off any parts that seem burnt. Top with one of the suggested toppings.
Plain (untopped) toasts will keep for several days in an airtight container.
Topping Idea Note:
Spread thin toasts with ricotta cheese, cream cheese, or cottage cheese.
Top it with a sprinkle of olive oil, salt and black pepper.
Use cold butter and a little salt.
Sliced or smooshed on hard boiled egg.
Hummus or bean paste.
Thin sliced tomatoes and basil leaves.
Smoked fish or thin slices of leftover grilled meat.
Salsa Verde.
Crumbled thin toasts also make great croutons.
