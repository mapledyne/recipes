Jerk-Spiced Chicken Hakka Noodles

Category : Meat, Pasta

Description:
Hakka noodles are very popular Chinese and Indian dishes. As a child growing up in Calcutta, India, I ate them all the time. For this variation, I added jerk-seasoned chicken (the recipe was given to me by my daughter's babysitter), which marries so well with the Indo-Chinese spiced Hakka noodles. It's full of fun and flavor.

Prep Time: 25 Min
Cook time: 25 Min
Servings:

Ingredients:
6 scallions
1 jalapeño
1 teaspoon allspice
1/2 tablespoon thyme, dried
1 tablespoon coriander, ground
2 teaspoons cinnamon, ground
1 teaspoon nutmeg, ground
1 teaspoon sugar, brown
1 1/2 teaspoons salt
1 teaspoon black pepper, freshly ground
1/4 cup vinegar, white
1 tablespoon neutral oil
1 pound chicken thighs, thinly sliced
3 tablespoons neutral oil, divided
1/2 teaspoon black pepper, freshly ground
1 1/2 cups cabbage, shredded
1/2 cup carrots, sliced
1 onion, small, diced
1 bell pepper, red, thinly sliced
8 scallions, chopped
1 1/2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
1 tablespoon vinegar, white
2 tablespoons ketchup
1 teaspoon Sriracha or other hot sauce
1 teaspoon ginger, fresh, grated
1 teaspoon garlic, finely minced
10 ounces Chinese noodles or egg noodles, cooked
1 1/2 teaspoons salt, or as needed

Instructions:
For the Jerk Chicken Marinade:
1. In a small bowl, whisk together all of the ingredients except the chicken thighs until it forms a thick purée.
2. Add the chicken pieces to the bowl and mix until the chicken is well-covered with marinade. Set aside.
For the Noodles:
3. In a large wok over medium heat, add 2 tablespoons of the oil and the black pepper and cook for 30 seconds.
4. Add the cabbage, carrots, onion, red pepper, and scallions and cook for about 10 minutes, or until the vegetables soften.
5. Meanwhile, in a separate small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, vinegar, ketchup, Sriracha, ginger, and garlic. Set aside.
6. Once the vegetables are cooked, remove them from the heat and transfer them to a medium bowl.
To Cook the Chicken and Assemble:
7. Add the remaining tablespoon of oil into the wok and add the sliced jerk spice chicken and pan-fry for about 8 to 10 minutes or until done.
8. Add in the vegetables, vinegar-ketchup sauce, and noodles and stir to thoroughly coat.
9. Check for salt and add as needed.
