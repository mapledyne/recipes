Fish Fillets, Pan-cooked Thin

Category : Kid Friendly, Meat, Seafood, Weeknight

Description:


Prep Time: 10 Min
Cook time: 10 Min
Servings: 4

Ingredients:
4 6oz fillets fish, flounder, sole, or other 1/4-inch-thick fish
salt
pepper
2 tablespoons olive oil, extra virgin
4 tablespoons butter, salted
all-purpose flour, for dredging
1/4 cup lemon juice, fresh
parsley, fresh, leaves chopped, for garnish
1 lemon, wedges, for serving

Instructions:
Warm 4 dinner plates in a 200* oven.
Sprinkle the fillets with salt and pepper.
Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat for 2-3 minutes. Add 1 tablespoon of the oil and 1 tablespoon of the butter. When butter foam subsides, dredge two of the fillets, one by one, in the flour, shaking off any excess, and add them to the he pan.
Raise the heat to high and cook the fillets until golden on each side, 4-5 minutes total.
Transfer to the warm serving plates and return to the oven if you like.
Wipe the pan and repeat with the remaining fillets.
Turn the heat down to medium and add the remaining butter to the pan. Cook until the butter foams, a minute or two. Add the lemon juice and cook, stirring and scrapping the bottom of the pan, for about 15 seconds.
Pour the sauce over the fillets.
Garnish with parsley and serve immediately with lemon wedges.
