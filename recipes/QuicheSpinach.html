Quiche, Spinach

Category : Breakfast, Casserole, Lunch, Vegetarian Main Dish, Weeknight

Description:
This was one of the first quiches I ever made, it remains one of my go-tos and I suspect it always will. One of my favorite parts about it is the accessibility of the ingredients. In some lucky cases, I already have them on hand, but that may just be because many of the ingredients are the core ones in my everyday cooking. It’s also one of these gorgeous recipes that’s impossible to break. I have made it with different cheeses and it still worked. For an Atkins-prone family member, I have baked it in a greased glass quiche pan without a crust. I have swapped the light cream with heavy, and gone in the opposite direction and used skim milk or even evaporated low fat milk. I have used red or white onions instead of scallions; I have added a smashed clove of garlic. I have par-baked the crust, but I generally don’t bother and don’t find it particularly soggy. I have used a homemade tart shell and I have used a store-bought one, but I will not admit which one I worked with yesterday.

Prep Time: 15 Min
Cook time: 25 Min
Servings:

Ingredients:
1 pie crust or puff pastry
1 3-ounce package cream cheese, room temperature
1/3 cup cream, half and half (or milk)
3 eggs
1 10-ounce package spinach, frozen chopped, thawed and drained
1/2 cup cheese, cheddar (gruyere works well too), grated
1/4 cup cheese, parmesan, grated
1/2 cup onion, red or white or shallots or 4 to 6 green onions, finely diced or thinly sliced
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon pepper

Instructions:
Preheat oven to 425°. Beat cream cheese in medium bowl until smooth. Gradually beat in half and half and eggs. Mix in remaining ingredients.
Pour mixture into prepared crust. Bake until crust is golden brown and filling is set, about 25 minutes. Cool 10 minutes before serving.
