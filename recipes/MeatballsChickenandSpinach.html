Meatballs, Chicken and Spinach

Category : Kid Friendly, Meat

Description:
An involved weekend recipe, but it doubles well if you have a 14-cup food processor, and leftovers re-heat well. Cook the meatballs in two batches if you double. Can be used with spaghetti sauce (instead of the sweet and sour sauce).

Prep Time: 30 Min
Cook time: 1 H
Servings: 4

Ingredients:
1 slice bread, rustic (Bakery Nouveau)
2 garlic cloves, large
2 ounces pepperoni, slices
12 ounces chicken thighs, boneless skinless
spinach, frozen, defrosted and squeezed dry
1 onion, small
1/4 teaspoon cinammon, ground
1/4 teaspoon salt, kosher, Diamond Crystal brand (exact)
a few grinds pepper black, freshly ground
1 tablespoon vinegar, red wine
1 ounce cheese, parmesan
zest of 1 lemon
1 egg, large
For the sauce:
1 tablespoon olive oil, extra-virgin
1/2 cup wine, white dry, sauvignon blanc or pinot grigio
2 teaspoons sugar
2 tablespoons vinegar, red wine
1 cup chicken broth
rice, for serving

Instructions:
1. Place the bread in a food processor and grind into crumbs. You should have about 1/2 cup. Remove to a separate large bowl.
2. Place the garlic and the pepperoni in the food processor and process until very finely chopped.
3. Add the chicken, spinach, onion, cinnamon, salt and pepper, and process until well mixed. Poke through with a fork or spoon looking for unincorporated chicken chunks, and process further until you don't find any. You're not looking for a mousse texture, but pretty close. Remove to the bowl with the bread crumbs.
4. Stir in 1 tablespoon vinegar, cheese, lemon zest and egg. With your hands, form the meat mixture into 2-inch meatballs, placing them in a single layer on a large plate. You may cover the meatballs with plastic wrap at this point and place them in the fridge for several hours.
5. Pour the olive oil into a 12-inch non-stick or cast iron skillet and heat over medium-high. Add the meatballs to the pan and cook until well browned on all sides, turning carefully with tongs. Turn the heat down if they are browning too fast. Cooking will take about 10 minutes. Pour off or blot any excess fat.
6. Return the heat to medium-high (if necessary) and add the wine. Cook until nearly completely reduced, about 2 minutes. Add the sugar, vinegar, and broth, bring to a simmer, reduce heat to low, and cover. Simmer 12 to 15 minutes, turning the meatballs once until the meatballs are firm and register between 165 degrees and 170 degrees Fahrenheit in the center.
7. Remove the meatballs to a bowl, raise the heat to medium-high, and boil the sauce down until it's sour, sweet and salty enough to your taste. Pour over meatballs and serve immediately with rice or noodles.
