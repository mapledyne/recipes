Everyday Bread

Category : Breads, Snacks

Description:


Prep Time:
Cook time:
Servings:

Ingredients:
Neutral vegetable oil, for greasing
1 tsp instant (rapid rise) yeast
3 1/2 cups water at room temperature
5 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
1 3/4 cups whole-wheat flour
1/3 cup flaxseeds (optional)
4 tsp kosher salt

Instructions:
1.Oil the inside of two 9 x 5 in. metal loaf pans.
2.In a large bowl, mix yeast, liquid, flours, seeds, and salt. (I put the yeast and liquid in last.) Scrape dough into pans.
3.Drape with a clean, damp dish towel and let rise until level with the top of the pans. Note: This can take as little as two hours or as long as five; don't worry too much about the time, as the bread will rise at its own pace. Many recipes specify plastic wrap to cover rising bread; I don' use it because a) it's plastic, and b) it never stays in place. Occasionally this dough rises extra high and sticks to the towel; just pull it off the best you can. In the prospect of this bothers you, cover with lightly oiled plastic wrap instead.
4.Preheat oven to 450 degrees F and bake for 30 minutes.
5.Remove bread from pans, return loaves to oven and bake directly on racks for 15 minutes more. The bread is done when richly colored and hollow when tapped.
6.Let it cool before slicing.
Store in a paper bag for up to a week or wrap tightly and freeze.
