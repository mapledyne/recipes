Eggs, Omelets

Category : Breakfast, Vegetarian Main Dish, Weeknight

Description:
Prefers one-egg small omelets.

Prep Time: 2 Min
Cook time: 2 Min
Servings: 1

Ingredients:
1 egg
1 tablespoon milk, (or water)
salt, to taste
pepper black, freshly ground, to taste
1/2 tablespoon butter
filling of your choice

Instructions:
Crack the egg into a bowl large enough to take some action, add the milk and season with salt and pepper. Whisk until completely mixed and streak free.
Heat a skillet just right to medium low, the butter should melt then foam gently. When it stops, add the egg. Tilt the skillet around so the egg swirls to cover it all.
When it's set all over with a liquidy part still left in the middle, add the filling to the side you're flipping onto, which will be the bottom on the omelet (for righties, the left hand side). Run a small spatula around the perimeter of the omelet to loosen the edges and then slip it under the unfilled. Fold it over the filling, turn off the heat, and cover with the pan lid for a couple of minutes. Take a peek inside, and when it's set the way you like it, tilt the skillet over a plate and use a spatula to slide it out.
Hold in a warm oven if you're not eating alone or are already hungry for more and need to make another.
Filling ideas:
-Feta cheese, plain or with scallions
-Cheddar cheese
-Goat cheese and fresh herbs
-Ricotta cheese and ratatouille
-Tomatoes sliced VERY thin with sour cream and cilantro
-Tomatoes sliced VERY thin with pesto
-Cooked fish with parsley and yogurt or sour cream
-Cooked rice or faro (pan-fried for a minute before you turn the heat to low and add the egg)
