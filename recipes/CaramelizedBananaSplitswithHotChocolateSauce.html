Caramelized Banana Splits with Hot Chocolate Sauce

Category : Dessert

Description:
With its warm, gooey goodness, this dessert will bring bananas Foster to mind. You'll have some sauce left over, which will be handy because, we assure you, you'll be in the mood to have this again the next nightâ€”if not sooner.

Prep Time:
Cook time: 20 Min
Servings: 4

Ingredients:
3 tablespoons butter, unsalted
3 bananas, not ripe til Monday, sliced diagonally 1/2 inch thick
1/2 cup sugar, packed light brown
1 cup cream, heavy
4 ounces chocolate, fine-quality bittersweet (no more than 60% cacao if marked), finely chopped
1 pint cinnamon or vanilla ice cream
1/2 cup walnuts, chopped, toasted and cooled

Instructions:
1. Melt butter in a 12-inch heavy skillet over medium heat, then add bananas in 1 layer and sprinkle with brown sugar. Increase heat to medium-high and cook bananas, gently turning once and shaking skillet occasionally, until sugar is caramelized, 3 to 4 minutes. Divide bananas among 4 bowls, leaving any remaining caramel in skillet.
2. Add cream to skillet and boil over medium heat, stirring, until caramel is dissolved. Add chocolate with a pinch of salt and whisk until melted.
3. Top bananas with scoops of ice cream, then drizzle with some of chocolate sauce and sprinkle with nuts. (There will be extra sauce.)
