Tomato and Corn Salad with Shallot Vinagrette

Category : Salad, Veggie Side

Description:
This is very very simple - only really works with fresh tomatoes and fresh corn when they are in season.

Prep Time: 15 Min
Cook time:
Servings: 5

Ingredients:
2 small or 1 medium shallot, minced
1 medium garlic clove, minced
5 tablespoons vinegar, red wine
1/2 cup olive oil, extra-virgin
1/8 teaspoon sugar
a good pinch salt, sea
5 basil leaves, fresh
2 large tomatoes, beefsteak
1 or 2 handfuls tomatoes, cherry or grape
1 ear corn on the cob, kernels cut off
to taste salt, flaky
a few basil leaves, fresh

Instructions:
Make the Vinagrette:
Stir together the shallots, garlic and vinegar in a small bowl. Set aside for 1 hour - or even a couple of hours if you have time. You want to soften the garlic and shallot to no longer taste raw and be slightly pickled.
Whisk in the olive oil, sugar and salt. Add the basil leaves, bashing them with a whisk to bruise them and release their oils. Let sit for another 30 minutes. Before using, remove and discard the basil leaves and taste for salt.
Assemble the Salad:
Slice the tomatoes into 1/2 inch thick rounds. Arrange them on a platter. Halve the cherry tomatoes and strew them around the tomato slices. Scatter the raw corn kernels on top. Season with a good pinch of flaky salt. Spoon the vinaigrette liberally over the whole platter 1/4 cup won't be too much - you'll want extra for eating with bread - and then snip the basil leaves into strips over the top.
Vinagrette will keep for 3 days (before garlic and shallot taste too strong).
Serve with buttery scrambled eggs, chewy bread and a wedge of sharp cheddar cheese.
